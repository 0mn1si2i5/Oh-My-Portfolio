## The premise

Personality tests are engaging because they give people a language for noticing themselves. NBTI asks whether that reflection can feel less like a diagnosis and more like looking into a set of historical mirrors: complex people, imperfect evidence, and no single answer that settles who someone was.

## The experience

The experience is mobile-first and intentionally direct. People move through 18 concrete scenarios and six calibration questions, then receive a result built from six action tendencies: a primary historical counterpart, nearby and contrasting matches, a short reading, and links to the source material behind the historical notes.

## The system behind it

I designed the product, interaction flow, visual direction, and the matching system, then built it as a static React site. A deterministic distance model compares each response vector with 32 character profiles, so every result is generated locally and repeatably—without an account, cookies, analytics, or saved answers.

## A careful use of history

NBTI is a playful cultural experience, not a psychological or scientific assessment. The historical figures are loose points of comparison rather than proof of a personality label; the result writing keeps the user’s present choices in focus and treats the past as material for reflection, not a verdict.
