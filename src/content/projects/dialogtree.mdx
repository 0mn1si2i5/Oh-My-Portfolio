<section lang="en" data-locale-content="en">

## What I noticed

Long LLM conversations are usually stored as a single, linear transcript. Once an exploration splits into alternatives, earlier decisions become hard to revisit, compare, or reuse without manually rebuilding context.

## What I explored

I explored whether a conversation could be treated as a navigable knowledge structure rather than a scrolling record. The question was not simply how to show more messages, but how to help people return to a thought, follow its context, and confidently branch from it.

## What I made

DialogTree turns conversations into an interactive tree. People can navigate paths, branch from any point, annotate meaningful nodes, and search across conversations using both text and semantic retrieval. The product pairs a D3-powered tree workspace with streaming chat, persistent conversation history, and retrieval-aware search.

## What I learned

The interaction became the basis for _Branchat: A Tree-Structured Interface for Efficient Revisitation in Long-Horizon LLM Conversations_, presented as an ACM CHI 2026 Poster. In a 12-participant study, the tree interface improved efficiency on complex information-revisitation tasks and reduced cognitive burden. DialogTree remains private work; the live demo is shared here, but its source code is not.

<section lang="zh-CN" data-locale-content="zh">

## 我注意到的问题

长期 LLM 对话通常被存为一条线性的记录。当探索分岔出不同可能性时，想要重新访问、比较或复用早先的决策，往往只能手动重建上下文。

## 我探索的方向

我探索能否将对话视为可导航的知识结构，而非不断滚动的记录。关键不只是展示更多信息，而是帮助人们回到一个想法、沿着其上下文继续探索，并有把握地从那里分支。

## 我做出的东西

DialogTree 将对话转化为可交互的树。人们可以沿路径导航、从任意节点分支、标注有意义的节点，并通过文本和语义检索在对话间搜索。产品结合了由 D3 驱动的树状工作区、流式聊天、持久化对话记录与具备检索意识的搜索。

## 我学到的事

这套交互最终成为 _Branchat: A Tree-Structured Interface for Efficient Revisitation in Long-Horizon LLM Conversations_ 的基础，并以 ACM CHI 2026 海报论文形式发表。在一项 12 人参与的研究中，树状界面提升了复杂信息回访任务的效率，并减轻了认知负担。DialogTree 仍是私人项目；这里公开了在线演示，但不公开源代码。
