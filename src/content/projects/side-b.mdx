## What I noticed

Sharing a song should be a small social gesture. Instead, the link often sends the recipient to the wrong platform, hides useful context, or makes the music impossible to reach.

## What I explored

Side B asks what a platform-agnostic music-sharing experience could feel like: a single link that first understands the song, then presents a useful, human-scale way to continue listening.

## What I made

I defined the core “parse — gather — share” flow, shaped the information architecture, and built a local iOS prototype in SwiftUI. The prototype is designed around the moments before and after a link is opened, with backend-service and music-platform integration considered as part of the product system.

## What I learned

The project is intentionally still a prototype. It is a place to test whether better link handling can make a familiar act of sharing feel more connected, rather than merely more compatible.
